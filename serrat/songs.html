Mediterráneo

Serrat composes a song to the Mediterranean Sea, linking it to both his own life and the influence he exerts on the countries he bathes. According to an urban legend Serrat may have composed the song during his time locked in the Monastery of Montserrat,along with other artists,in protest at the Burgos Process in the late 1970s.

Cantares

The song was dedicated to the poet Antonio Machado. The song, one of the most successful on the album, made the Guild of Booksellers of Madrid express to Serrat its appreciation in a letter in which its representatives thank Serrat for having helped to revive interest in Machado's work, referring directly to the increase in the sales of books of the poet who brought with him the promotion and popularization of his texts through the voice of Serrat.
